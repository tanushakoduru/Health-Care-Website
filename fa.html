First-Aid Information

On this page, you will find valuable information about first-Aid in various emergency situations. Proper precautions can make a easy path in an emergency. Here are some first-aid tips:

Cuts and Scrapes

Scrapes, unlike cuts, are not deep wounds, but they're still painful because they can cover a large, sensitive area of skin. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hands before handling the wound.
  • Clean the wound gently with cool, clean water and mild soap.
  • Apply an over-the-counter antiseptic ointment to help prevent infection.
  • Cover the wound with a clean bandage or sterile gauze to keep it clean.
Burns

burn affects both the epidermis and the second layer of skin (dermis).Here are some tips:

  • For minor burns, immediately hold the burn under cool (not cold) running water for about 10-15 minutes to help alleviate pain and prevent further damage.
  • Avoid using ice on burns as it can cause more damage.
  • Cover the burn with a sterile non-stick bandage or clean cloth.
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen can help manage pain.
Sprains and Strains

Symptoms include pain, swelling, bruising, and being unable to move your joint. Here are some tips:

  • Rest the injured area to prevent further damage.
  • Apply ice wrapped in a cloth for 15-20 minutes every 1-2 hours during the first 48 hours.
  • Compress the area with an elastic bandage to reduce swelling.
  • Elevate the injured limb if possible to reduce swelling.
Poisoning

The symptoms caused by poisoning depend on the poison, the amount taken, and the age and underlying health of the person who takes it.

  • Call emergency services immediately if someone is poisoned.
  • Do not try to induce vomiting unless advised by a medical professional.
  • Provide information about the substance ingested.
Nosebleeds

Epistaxis” is the medical term for a nosebleed, which is a loss of blood from the tissue that lines the inside of your nose

  • Have the person sit down and lean forward slightly.
  • Pinch the nostrils together just below the bony part of the nose.
  • Maintain pressure for about 10-15 minutes.
  • Avoid tilting the head back, as this can cause blood to flow down the throat.
Minor Allergic Reactions

Mild reaction​​ Symptoms of a mild allergic reaction can include: hives, or itchy red spots on the skin. rash.

  • If you suspect an allergic reaction, take an over-the-counter antihistamine (if not allergic to it).
  • If there's difficulty breathing or a severe reaction, seek medical attention immediately.
