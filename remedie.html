• Cool Water: Hold the burned area under cool (not cold) running water for about 10-20 minutes. Avoid using ice-cold water, as it can damage the tissue further.

  • Aloe Vera: Applying aloe vera gel to the burn can promote healing and reduce pain. Make sure you're using pure aloe vera gel, not a product with added colors or fragrances.

  • Honey: Honey has natural antibacterial properties and can be applied to minor burns. Use a sterile gauze pad to apply a thin layer of honey to the affected area.

  • Vinegar: Diluted vinegar (apple cider vinegar or white vinegar) can be applied to a burn using a clean cloth. Vinegar has antiseptic properties and can help prevent infection.

  • Turmeric Paste: Turmeric has natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. Mix turmeric powder with water to form a paste. Apply this paste to the wound and cover it with a clean bandage. Turmeric can stain clothing, so be cautious.

  • Baking Soda Paste: Mix baking soda with a small amount of water to create a paste. Apply the paste to the wound and cover it with a bandage. Baking soda helps reduce inflammation and itching.

  • Saltwater Solution: Dissolve salt in warm water to create a saline solution. Use this solution to clean the wound. Saline solution helps prevent infection and promotes healing.

  • Coconut Oil: Coconut oil has antimicrobial properties and can keep the wound moisturized. Apply a small amount of coconut oil to the cut or scrape and cover it with a bandage.
